# Portfolio

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters and hiring managers evaluating technical fit. They spend 6-10 seconds scanning before deciding to move forward. Each role page is a standalone link they reach from a resume — they may never see any other page.

## Product Purpose

A multi-role portfolio where each role (Data Scientist, Product Manager, Data Analyst) lives on its own independent subpage with a shareable URL. Serves as the digital face across three distinct career tracks, with a hub page tying them together. Success: recruiters open the link and immediately see competence, outcomes, and fit for that specific role.

## Brand Personality

Confident, precise, articulate, brief. The voice of someone who can actually make things happen — no fluff, no filler, every pixel earns its place.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI-slop templates (same cards, same gradients, same patterns)
- Over-designed / ornamental for its own sake
- Corporate sterile / boilerplate portfolio language
- "Eyebrow" section headers (ABOUT / WORK / CONTACT in tiny uppercase)
- Side-stripe borders, gradient text, glassmorphism as default

## Design Principles

- **Substance over ornament** — Every element has a job. If it doesn't communicate capability, it doesn't belong.
- **Speed is a feature** — Zero unnecessary JS. Lighthouse 100 is the floor, not the goal.
- **Independent narratives** — Each role page tells its own story. A recruiter for one role should never feel like they're on the wrong page.
- **Think different** — Avoid every pattern that reads as "AI made this." Originality in structure, not decoration.
- **Brevity** — Fewer words, more impact. Recruiters scan; give them the signal, skip the noise.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA compliance. Reduced motion respected. Color contrast ≥4.5:1 body, ≥3:1 large text. No information conveyed by color alone.
